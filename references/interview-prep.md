# Interview Preparation

Trigger this reference when the candidate receives an interview invitation.

---

## Within 24 Hours of Receiving the Invitation

### 1. Confirm logistics
- Date, time, timezone
- Format: phone / video / in-person / panel
- Who will be interviewing (ask if not stated — LinkedIn their names)
- Duration
- Any prep materials or case studies requested

### 2. Re-read the JD
The JD is the interview blueprint. Every bullet point is a potential question.
- Circle the 3–5 most-emphasised requirements
- For each, identify your best real example that demonstrates it
- Note anything you flagged as a gap — prepare to address it honestly

### 3. Review your application materials
Re-read the exact resume version and cover letter you submitted. The interviewer is reading the same document — your answers should match what's written.

## STAR Framework for Behavioural Questions

Most competency questions ("Tell me about a time when...") are answered best with:

| Element | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **S**ituation | What was the context? (1–2 sentences) |
| **T**ask | What were you responsible for? |
| **A**ction | What did **you** specifically do? (not "we") |
| **R**esult | What was the outcome, and how do you know? |

Keep each story to 90–120 seconds spoken. The Result must include a metric or a concrete outcome.

Prepare 5–6 STAR stories before the interview. Good ones are reusable across multiple question types.

## Questions by Interview Type

### HR / recruiter screen (typically 30 min)
Common questions:
- "Tell me about yourself" → 60-second career narrative, ending with why this role
- "Why this company?" → use Phase 4 company research
- "What are you looking for in your next role?" → align to what the JD offers
- "What's your salary expectation?" → see `references/salary-negotiation.md`

### Hiring manager interview (typically 45–60 min)
Expect:
- Competency / behavioural questions based on JD requirements
- "Walk me through your experience with [X]" → pick most relevant role
- Case or situational questions for senior roles
- "What questions do you have for us?" → always prepare 3 (see below)

### Panel interview
- Address all panellists, not just the one who asked
- Slightly longer answers (more people to satisfy)
- Note each panellist's role — tailor your answer angle to their function

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

Always prepare at least 3. Good questions signal genuine interest and strategic thinking.

**About the role:**
- "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- "What's the biggest challenge the person in this role will face?"

**About the team:**
- "How would you describe the working style of the team?"
- "What made the last person in this role successful?" (or "Why is this role open?")

**About the company:**
- "Where is the organisation headed in the next 12–18 months?"
- "What's something about working here that doesn't show up on the website?"

**Avoid**: salary questions in early rounds (unless they raise it), questions easily answered by the website, anything with a negative framing.

## Post-Interview Follow-up

Send a thank-you email within **24 hours**:

```
Subject: Thank you — [Role Title] interview

Hi [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I left the conversation even more excited about [specific thing discussed] and confident that my experience in [area] would add value to [team/project mentioned].

Looking forward to next steps.

[Name]
[Phone] | [Email]
```

Length: 4–6 sentences. Reference one specific thing from the conversation — this proves you were listening and differentiates you from candidates who send generic notes.

---

## Red Flags During the Interview (for the candidate)

The interview is also an evaluation of the company. Note:
- Vague answers about team structure or role scope
- Unable to describe what success looks like
- Negative language about previous employees
- Pressure to decide quickly or skip reference checks
- Inconsistency between what the JD said and what they describe

These are worth factoring into the offer decision.
