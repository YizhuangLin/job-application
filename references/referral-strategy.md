# Referral & Network Strategy

Internal referrals convert to interviews at 5–10× the rate of cold applications. Check this channel **before** cold-applying to any High Match company.

---

## Step 1 — Map Your Network to Target Companies

For each shortlisted company, ask the candidate:

1. Do you know anyone who works there, or has worked there in the past 2 years?
2. Do you know anyone who might know someone there? (second-degree)
3. Are there alumni from your school or previous employers at that company? (check LinkedIn "Alumni" filter)

Even a second-degree connection is worth a message.

---

## Step 2 — How to Ask for a Referral

Draft a short, direct message. Do not ask for a job — ask for information or an introduction.

**To someone you know well:**
```
Hi [Name],

I saw [Company] is hiring for [Role] — it looks like a great fit for my background in [relevant area]. Would you be comfortable putting in a referral, or connecting me with the right person on the team? Totally understand if it's not a good time.

[Name]
```

**To a weaker connection (e.g. LinkedIn alumni):**
```
Hi [Name],

I hope you don't mind the cold message — I'm [name], a [title] who [1-line relevant background]. I noticed [Company] is hiring for [Role] and your experience there caught my eye. Would you have 10 minutes to share what it's like working there? I'd really appreciate your perspective before I apply.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Never open with "Can you refer me?" — earn the ask first
- Keep it under 80 words
- Give them an easy out ("totally understand if not")
- Follow up once after 5 days if no response; then let it go

---

## Step 3 — LinkedIn Warm Application

Even without a personal connection, a LinkedIn message to the hiring manager or recruiter before submitting can increase visibility:

```
Hi [Name],

I just applied for the [Role] position and wanted to put a face to the application. I've been following [Company]'s work in [area] and believe my background in [specific skill + metric] is a strong match. Happy to answer any questions.

[Name]
```

Send this **within 24 hours of applying**. Keep it under 60 words. Do not attach anything.

---

## When Referral Isn't Possible

If no connection exists, cold-apply but prioritise:
1. Speed (within 48 hours of posting)
2. Quality of customisation (Phase 5)
3. Company research depth (Phase 4)
4. A well-written cover note (Phase 6)

The referral path replaces some of this friction — when it's available, use it first.
