# Company Research Framework

Do this before customising the resume or writing cover material. The goal is to answer:
**"What does this company need right now, and how does the candidate map to that need?"**

---

## Research Sources (in priority order)

1. **The JD itself** — tone, requirements, and what's emphasised tell you a lot about the company's maturity and pain point
2. **Company LinkedIn page** — size, recent hires, team composition, recent posts
3. **Company website** — about page, recent blog posts, product/service offerings
4. **News search** — `[Company name] site:techcrunch.com OR site:betakit.com OR site:theglobeandmail.com` (last 6 months)
5. **Glassdoor** — culture signals, management quality, common complaints (read critically — selection bias)
6. **LinkedIn recruiter/hiring manager** — if the JD names a contact or you can identify the hiring manager

---

## What to Extract

### Company stage & size

| Stage | Signals | What it means for candidate |
|---|---|---|
| Early startup (< 30 people) | "wear many hats", "scrappy", "fast-paced", equity mention | Expect ambiguity; emphasise autonomy and breadth |
| Scale-up (30–300) | "growing team", "series A/B", process-building language | Emphasise impact metrics and the ability to build |
| Enterprise (300+) | Structured processes, compliance, "stakeholder management" | Emphasise coordination, reporting, and reliability |
| Non-profit / gov | Mission language, budget constraints | Emphasise mission alignment and resource efficiency |

### Why this role exists (infer from JD language)

| Language pattern | Likely reason | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| "Build from scratch", "establish" | New function | Emphasise initiative and zero-to-one experience |
| "Grow and scale", "expand" | Growth hire | Emphasise scale, volume, or geographic expansion |
| "Support the team", "assist" | Backfill (someone left) | Emphasise reliability and smooth handoff capability |
| "Improve existing", "optimise" | Succession or performance gap | Emphasise analytical and improvement skills |

### Recent signals to look for

- **Funding announced** → growth mode, likely adding headcount across functions
- **Leadership change** → new direction, new team rebuilding
- **Product launch** → need for specialists in that area
- **Restructuring / layoffs** → roles may be stretched; ask about team size carefully

---

## What to Do With This Research

### In the Summary
Adjust the framing to match the company's stage:
- Startup: "...delivering results with limited resources and fast iteration cycles"
- Scale-up: "...scaling [function] from early-stage to [metric]"
- Enterprise: "...managing cross-functional programmes across [scope]"

### In the Cover Letter opening
Reference something specific:
- ❌ Generic: "I have long admired [Company]'s work in the industry."
- ✅ Specific: "I saw that [Company] recently launched [product/expansion] — that's exactly the kind of challenge where my [specific experience] would have direct impact."

### In application form answers
"Why do you want to work here?" — Answer with company-specific context, not generic enthusiasm. Reference the company's stage, recent news, or mission.

### In the skills emphasis decision
If the company is data-driven → surface analytics keywords more prominently.  
If the company is brand-led → surface creative and content keywords more prominently.  
If the company is technical → surface technical depth over breadth.

---

## Time Budget

This research should take **10–15 minutes per company**, not hours. The goal is enough context to be credibly specific — not exhaustive due diligence. Prioritise High Match companies; for Medium Match, do a lighter 5-minute scan.
